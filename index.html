About

I am currently a student at Wilfrid Laurier University where I am completing an MSc thesis in Mathematics. I did my undergrad at University of Waterloo, where I completed a degree in Mathematical Physics. I also completed a minor in Pure mathematics and a specialization in Astrophysics. I typically like to work on physical and geometric problems, as well as data-driven problems. My masters project focuses on Greens' functions for the Laplace-Beltrami operator on a manifold. I am trying to understand relationships between locally symmetric spaces and radial symmetry of Green's functions. I am also studying the N-body problem on manifolds, and trying to understand the resulting Hamiltonian dynamics on complex manifolds, specifically Kahler manifolds. I am well versed in computer programming with Python, C++, Java, and more. I am interested in machine learning models for gravitational wave processing. A problem which interests me is the stochastic gravitational wave background radiation, and whether measurements of it can provide cosmological information. I am interested in problems of causality, time, and information. I like to think about relationships between geometry and physics, particularly symplectic and complex geometry. A series of blog posts by John Baez has caught my attention, which can be found here. The blog posts describe a connection between information geometry, thermodynamics and statistical mechanics. I would be interested in pursuing projects related to this. I am interested in fluid mechanics and nonlinear partial differential equations in general. I have worked on geometric control theory results for microhydrodynamics. I would be interested in researching more geometric approaches to fluid mechanics.
