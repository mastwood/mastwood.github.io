Coursework

This is a list of all the upper division coursework I've completed at UWaterloo (for use in grad school applications). Starred courses are in progress.

Graduate Courses

  • PHYS701: Quantum Mechanics. Taught by Crystal Senko. Heisenberg and interaction pictures of quantum mechanics. Addition of angular momentum. Spherical tensor operators and the Wigner-Eckhart Theorem. Time dependent perturbation theory, the variatonal method, and the WKB approximation. The Aharanov-Bohm effect and geometric phase. Systems of identical particles with spin. Quantization of the electromagnetic field.
  • *PHYS785: Quantum Field Theory for Cosmology. Taught by Achim Kempf. Quantization of Klein-Gordon field. Schrodinger and Fock representations of quantum field theory. Bogolubov transformations. Driving currents. Light-matter interaction. Unruh effect and detectors. Functional differentiation. Riemannian and Lorentzian structures. Einstein action and quantization. Darboux theorem. Free QFT on hyperbolic spacetime. Conservation and covariance of CCRs. Stone-Von Neumann theorem. Klein Gordon field in FRW spacetimes. Adiabatic vacuum. De Sitter horizon and inflation. Field fluctuation spectrum in expanding spacetime. Mukhanov variable and tensor polarizations. Schwarzchild spacetime. Boulware and Kruskal vacua. Hawking radiation. Path integral approach. Feynman diagrams.
  • *PMATH965: Topics in Geometry and Topology - Gauge Theory. Taught by Ruxandra Moraru. Vector bundles and principal bundles (definitions and basic constructions); connections, curvature, and gauge groups. Covariant derivatives and holonomy. Sheaves and sheaf cohomology. Characteristic classes and Chern Weil Theory. Flat connections and representations of the fundamental group. Metric connections on vector bundles. Some important equations of gauge theory: Yang-Mills, anti-self-dual, Hermitian-Einstein. Moduli spaces of Hermitian-Einstein connections. Kobiyashi-Hitchin Correspondence. Stable Holomorphic Bundles.

Physics

  • PHYS334: Quantum Physics II. Taught by Crystal Senko. Formalism of quantum mechanics. Operator approach to the harmonic oscillator. Quantum mechanics in three dimensions: Hydrogen atom, angular momentum and spin. Time-independent perturbation theory. Fine structure of hydrogen. Zeeman effect.
  • PHYS375: Stars. Taught by Avery Broderick. Stellar distances, masses, ages. Stellar interiors and atmospheres, the Saha Equation. Stellar equations of structure. Star formation and evolution. Cepheid Variables. Supernovae, white dwarfs, neutron stars, black holes.
  • PHYS358: Thermal Physics. Taught by Karen Cummings. Temperature and thermodynamic equilibrium. Work, internal energy and heat; first law, with examples. Kinetic theory of gases. Basic probability theory. Microscopic states and entropy. Absolute temperature, reversibility and the second law. Thermodynamic Functions and Maxwell's relations. Chemical potentials and free energy. Phase transitions.
  • *PHYS359: Statistical Mechanics. Taught by Jeff Chen. Phase space of quantum systems. Microcanonical ensemble. Paramagnets and ideal gases. Canonical ensemble. Free energy. Partition function. Classical and quantum ideal gases. Quantum paramagnetic systems. The Gibbs Paradox and multiparticle quantum physics. Grand Canonical ensemble and chemical potentials. Semiclassical correction to ideal gas. Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac distributions. Ideal electron gases and Fermi energy. Black body radiation. Bose-Einstein condensates. Virial coefficients.
  • PHYS363: Intermediate Classical Mechanics. Taught by Chris O'Donovan. Coupled oscillations and normal modes. Rigid Body Dynamics. Non-inertial frames of reference. Calculus of variations and d'Alambert's principle. Lagrangian mechanics (including rigid body mechanics and tops). Hamiltonian dynamics, the Poisson bracket, canonical transformations. Symplectomorphisms. Relativistic Lagrangian dynamics.
  • PHYS342: Electricity and Magnetism II. Taught by Matteo Mariantoni. Magnetic fields, Ampere's law, induced electromotive forces, magnetic devices, magnetic properties of materials, inductance, Electromagnetic waves and Poynting's theorem.

Pure Mathematics

  • PMATH347: Groups and Rings. Taught by Matt Satriano. Groups, subgroups, homomorphisms and quotient groups, isomorphism theorems, group actions, Cayley and Lagrange theorems, permutation groups and the fundamental theorem of finite abelian groups. Elementary properties of rings, subrings, ideals, homomorphisms and quotients, isomorphism theorems, polynomial rings, and unique factorization domains. Noetherian rings.
  • PMATH365: Differential Geometry. Taught by Spiro Karigiannis. Grassman algebra. Submanifolds of Euclidean n-space. Vector fields and differential forms. Integration on submanifolds and Stokes's Theorem. Hodge Star operator and vector calculus. De Rham Cohomology.
  • PMATH465: Smooth Manifolds. Taught by Stephen New. Topology of manifolds. Abstract manifolds, submanifolds. Submersions, immersions, and embeddings. Vector fields and differential forms. Abstract vector bundles. Lie groups and Lie algebras. Tensors. Lie Derivatives and Flows. De Rham Cohomology.
  • PMATH333: Introduction to Real Analysis. Taught by Stephen New. Axiomatic set theory. Properties of the real numbers. Metric space topology of the real numbers. Sequences and series. The Riemann integral.
  • *PMATH352: Complex Analysis. Taught by Spiro Karigiannis. Greens' Theorem and identities. Analytic functions, Cauchy-Riemann equations, Goursat's theorem, Cauchy's theorems, Morera's theorem, Liouville's theorem, maximum modulus principle. Harmonic functions, Schwarz's lemma, isolated singularities, Laurent series, residue theorem. Conformal maps.

Applied Mathematics

  • AMATH331: Applied Real Analysis. Taught by Blake Madill. Topology of Euclidean spaces, continuity, norms, completeness. Contraction mapping principle. Fourier series. Various applications, for example, to ordinary differential equations, optimization and numerical approximation.
  • AMATH351: Ordinary Differential Equations II. Taught by Xinzhi Liu. Second order linear differential equations with non-constant coefficients, Sturm comparison, oscillation and separation theorems, series solutions and special functions. Linear vector differential equations in Rn, an introduction to dynamical systems. Laplace transforms applied to linear vector differential equations, transfer functions, the convolution theorem. Green's functions. Sturm-Liouville Theory. Stability of inhomogenous and nonlinear systems.
  • *AMATH353: Partial Differential Equations I. Taught by Francis Poulin. Second order linear partial differential equations - the diffusion equation, wave equation, and Laplace's equation. Classification of second order partial differential equations. Methods of solution - separation of variables and eigenfunction expansions, the Fourier transform. Emphasis on Sturm-Liouville theory and differential operators. Physical interpretation of solutions in terms of diffusion, waves and steady states. First order non-linear partial differential equations and the method of characteristics.
